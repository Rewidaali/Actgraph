ActGraph: Environment-Aware Human Action Perception from Unconstrained Videos via Semantic Scene Graphs for Robot Imitation

Abstract

From unscripted human videos to robot-executable actions.

Understanding manipulation skills from unstructured videos is a fundamental challenge for enabling autonomous system design. Existing approaches rely on low-level visual features or language descriptions, often lacking temporal consistency and robustness to viewpoint changes.

We propose a semantic-consistent perception framework that transforms raw human videos into structured representations: keyframes, object-centric scene graphs, and semantic action sequences. The framework maintains object identities consistent under camera motion and scene transitions; its scene graphs evolve over time and explicitly encode spatial and interaction relationships, enabling robust skill extraction.

We evaluated the method on five complex, multi-view, unconstrained manipulation recordings. The structured outputs supported downstream task understanding by generating executable manipulation behaviors consistent with the original human videos.
